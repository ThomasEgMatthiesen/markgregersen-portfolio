ecoegoe

Mark Ibsgaard Gregersen's ecoegoe is an alluring, repetitive and multilayered organic music embracing inspirations spanning from early minimalism to improvised music, as well as modern produced music and sample technique. During the ensemble's early development, the Danish composer/producer/bassist came across acoustic and interplayed associations resembling electronic manipulation and effects. These findings sparked an aesthetic pursuit towards, and an expanding of a both meta-physical and technical intersection between interplayed music and electronically produced music.

ecoegoe's debut album will be released on vinyl in the summer and features both dry ensemble sound as well as post-produced and further composed parts. An ambiguous and tactile mix of sampled and interplayed material stretches out throughout the record and forms an almost ritualistic and hypnotic state.

“ecoegoe developed as i wanted to explore ways of navigating and controlling an egalitarian interplayed music that I had in mind. By embodying dense structures within this seven-piece ensemble, we found ways for us to sustain a multilayered soundscape, in which the sum of our activity causes narratives to unfold organically.”

Performing as an amplified seven piece ensemble ecoegoe's concerts are loud and intense while also containing the fragility and closeness of an interplaying “acoustic” ensemble. Additional production and sound design expands their sonic dimension and enhances their defying of categories.
